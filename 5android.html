Introduction

Welcome to Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, where we work to protect and conserve sea turtles.The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project in the Sri Lankan seaside town of Kosgoda. The hatchery was founded in 1981 in order to save the country's turtle population from extinction.

The hatchery takes turtle eggs that have been laid on neighboring beaches and keeps them safe until they hatch. The newborn turtles are then returned to the sea. The hatchery also provides medical treatment and rehabilitation to damaged and sick turtles before releasing them back into the wild.

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is also an educational center in addition to its conservation initiatives. Visitors can learn about the various turtle species native to Sri Lanka as well as the risks they face. In addition, the hatchery performs research.

Threats to Sea Turtles

Sea turtles face many threats, including:

  • Predation of nests by animals such as dogs and raccoons
  • Incidental capture in fishing gear
  • Pollution of their habitats

Conservation Efforts

At Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, we take several measures to protect sea turtles and their habitats:

  • Patrolling the beach to protect nests and relocate them if necessary
  • Releasing rehabilitated sea turtles back into the wild
  • Conducting educational programs to raise awareness about sea turtle conservation
