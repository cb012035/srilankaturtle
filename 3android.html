Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Welcome to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, a sanctuary for sea turtles where you can learn about conservation efforts and see turtles up close. Our hatchery is dedicated to protecting endangered sea turtles and raising awareness about the importance of their conservation.

At the hatchery, you can witness the process of turtle conservation, from incubation to release, and learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. You can also participate in our hatchery's efforts by adopting a turtle or making a donation to support our conservation work.

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist attraction located in the town of Hikkaduwa on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka. As part of our commitment to turtle conservation, we protect nesting sites, hatch eggs in a safe environment, and release baby turtles into the sea. Our hatchery is open to visitors who want to learn about sea turtle conservation and witness the process firsthand.

During your visit, you'll have the opportunity to see baby turtles up close, and if you're lucky, witness the release of newly hatched turtles into the sea. Our knowledgeable staff will guide you through the different stages of the turtle life cycle and provide information about the threats facing these magnificent creatures.

We also offer visitors the chance to participate in our conservation efforts through volunteer work or sponsoring a turtle release. Additionally, we sell souvenir items made from recycled materials and turtle shells, with proceeds going to support our conservation work.

While we take pride in our efforts to protect sea turtles, it's important to note that some critics have raised concerns about the hatchery's practices. We want to assure you that we are a legitimate conservation effort and take the welfare of our turtles very seriously. We continually review our practices and make improvements where necessary. We encourage visitors to do some research and make an informed decision before visiting us.
