About Turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is home to five species of sea turtles, all of which are endangered. Turtle hatcheries have been established in several areas along the coast to protect these species and their eggs from predators and human activities. Here's some information on turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka:

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery: Located in the town of Kosgoda, this hatchery is one of the most popular in Sri Lanka. It was established in 1981 with the aim of protecting sea turtles and their eggs. The hatchery provides a safe environment for turtle eggs to hatch and for the baby turtles to grow before being released into the ocean. Visitors can see the hatchery and learn about the different species of turtles found in Sri Lanka.

Bentota Turtle Hatchery: This hatchery is located in the town of Bentota and was established in 1986. The hatchery's main goal is to protect the eggs and hatchlings of sea turtles that are threatened by human activities such as beach erosion and commercial fishing. Visitors can see the hatchery and learn about the different species of turtles found in Sri Lanka.

Rekawa Turtle Conservation Project: This conservation project is located in the village of Rekawa and was established in 1996. The project aims to protect sea turtles and their nesting sites along the Rekawa beach. The project also monitors the hatching of turtle eggs and helps baby turtles make their way safely to the ocean. Visitors can see the project and learn about the different species of turtles found in Sri Lanka.

Sea Turtle Farm & Hatchery: This hatchery is located in the town of Habaraduwa and was established in 1986. The hatchery's main goal is to protect the eggs and hatchlings of sea turtles that are threatened by human activities such as beach erosion and commercial fishing. Visitors can see the hatchery and learn about the different species of turtles found in Sri Lanka.

These hatcheries provide important protection for Sri Lanka's endangered sea turtles, and they also serve as educational centers for visitors to learn about these amazing animals and the threats they face.

Green Sea Turtle

Name: Green Sea Turtle

Map: High population densities in beaches along the Pacific and Atlantic coasts

Description: Green Sea Turtles are one of the largest species of sea turtles and can be found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world. They are named for the color of their body fat, which is green due to their herbivorous diet.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Name: Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Map: High population densities in beaches along the Gulf of Mexico and Atlantic coasts

Description: Loggerhead Sea Turtles are a large species of sea turtle and are found in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. They are named for their large heads, which are adapted for crushing their prey of shellfish and crustaceans.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Name: Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Map: High population densities in beaches along the Caribbean coast

Description: Hawksbill Sea Turtles are a medium-sized species of sea turtle and are found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world. They are named for their narrow, pointed beaks, which are adapted for feeding on sponges.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Name: Leatherback Sea Turtle

Map: High population densities in beaches around the world

Description: Leatherback Sea Turtles are the largest species of sea turtle and are found in all oceans around the world. They are named for their unique leathery shell, which lacks the hard bony plates found in other sea turtles. Leatherbacks are known for their long migrations between nesting and foraging grounds, and for their ability to dive to great depths in search of their preferred prey: jellyfish.
