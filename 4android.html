Plastic Pollution

One of the biggest threats to turtles is plastic pollution. Turtles mistake plastic bags and other debris for food, and can become sick or even die from ingesting it. Plastic can also entangle turtles, making it difficult for them to swim and hunt.

To prevent plastic pollution, we can reduce our use of single-use plastics, properly dispose of waste, and participate in beach cleanups.

Poaching

Turtles are often poached for their meat, eggs, and shells. This can have a devastating effect on turtle populations, especially when combined with habitat destruction and other threats.

To prevent poaching, we can support organizations that work to protect turtles and their habitats, and raise awareness about the importance of conservation. Check out SEE Turtles for more information on turtle conservation and how to get involved.

Climate Change

Climate change can affect turtles in many ways, including by altering nesting and hatching patterns, changing ocean currents and temperatures, and affecting food sources.

To prevent the impacts of climate change on turtles, we can reduce our carbon footprint by using renewable energy sources, supporting policies that address climate change, and advocating for strong international action on climate. Check out Climate Council for more information on climate change and how to take action.
